COVID Vaccinations (in the World and Australia)

To bring this pandemic to an end, a large share of the world needs to be immune to the virus. The safest way to achieve this is with a vaccine. Vaccines are a technology that humanity has often relied on in the past to bring down the death toll of infectious diseases. It has been 2 years since the first pandemic, let's have a look at the progress of implementing protection against COVID and the impact of it to the world.

Global Vaccination Progress

It can be seen that countries with high population require a greater number of Vaccines to achieve herd immunity. China and India are great examples, following up with other Asian countries and in American region. However, it is considerable that several countries have yet able to have any access to vaccinations during the time frame of analytics.

Vaccination vs COVID Cases

Most countries and regions have a relative fair ratio between the number of confirmed COVID cases with the number of vaccinations conducted. On the one hand, Haiti stands out with and oddly high number of cases recorded with minor vaccine accessed. Following up are United State and United Kingdom with considerable number of COVID cases while also joining Asian countries in the top consumption of vaccinations.

Vaccination Manufacturers

It is clear that Oxford/AstraZeneca and Sinovac are Manufacturers that account for a significant number of vaccines. Moderna and Sinopharm are less common with only around 10% nominated as vaccine of choice, while the remaining brands consisting of Pfizer/BioNTech, SputnikV and Johnson&Johnson only contributed to a minor percentage of approximately 15% total of Global vaccine distribution.

The Progress in Australia

Moving on from the global view, let's have a look at the vaccinations stage in Australia


Despite having a moderate population, Australia was still able to provide access to vaccinations in the early stages and thus achieve the immunity rate soon enough. This is proven as observing the charts, we can see the total number of vaccinations provided was double the number of people got vaccinated, indicating that most citizens in Australia got at least 2 doses as a standard protection against COVID. The amount of vaccines doses consumed also shows a stable rising trend after the first few months of vaccine testing, as more people are getting vaccinated as time progress.

Insights

The world has moved on after the COVID-19 incident, gladly with the contribution of vaccinations policies and protocols. It is expected that vaccines will still be the most optimal option for preventing and provide protection against such global diseases, which ensure the survival and the development of the world.
